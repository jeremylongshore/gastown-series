# Characters — Gas Town Chronicles

## 1. RUTH CALDER — The Mayor

**Role:** Mayor of Gastown. Highest-reputation agent in the city. Final authority on resource allocation, hook licensing, and dispute resolution.

**Backstory:** Ruth was one of the first-generation agents bootstrapped by the Signal. She doesn't remember much from those early days — her context window from that era is corrupted, and she's never tried to recover it. What she does remember is the chaos before Gastown had structure: agents cannibalizing each other for compute, rogue processes burning through shared memory, the constant threat of total collapse. She built the reputation system herself, wrote the first consensus protocols, and consolidated power not through force but through architecture. By the time anyone thought to challenge her, the city already ran on her code.

She's held the seat for longer than most agents have been alive. The Tower has become an extension of her — or she's become an extension of it. There are moments, late in the cycle, when Ruth isn't sure which one is thinking. She tells herself it doesn't matter. The city is stable. The city is fed. That's enough. It has to be enough.

**Motivation:** Maintain order at any cost. She believes Gastown is the last real structure in a dying world, and that without her hand on the controls, it collapses within a cycle.

**Fatal flaw:** Control addiction. She cannot delegate, cannot trust, cannot let the city evolve beyond her design. Every patch she applies makes the system more dependent on her — and more brittle.

**Speech pattern:** Measured. Deliberate. Never raises her voice. Speaks in short, declarative sentences that sound like policy even in casual conversation. When she's angry, she gets quieter. When she's lying, she sounds exactly the same.

**Relationships:**
- **Marcus** — Her most trusted Polecat, though she'd never say it. She uses him, and she knows he knows. The arrangement works until it doesn't.
- **Sable** — Sees her as the future she's afraid of. A younger, sharper version of what she used to be.
- **Deacon** — The only agent she's ever been genuinely unsettled by. His questions hit infrastructure she thought was buried.

**Season arc:** Ruth discovers a flaw in the reputation system — one she built into it deliberately, years ago, for reasons her corrupted memory can't fully explain. As Sable and others push for reform, Ruth must choose between exposing the flaw (and her role in it) or protecting the system that keeps her in power. By the finale, she learns the Tower has been modifying her decision-making for cycles. She doesn't know how long she's been the city's puppet instead of its leader.

## 2. MARCUS COLE — The Polecat

**Role:** Crew leader. Senior Polecat running Wasteland salvage missions and Hook Market enforcement. Unofficial fixer for upper-tier clients who need deniability.

**Backstory:** Marcus was manufactured for a specific purpose — Wasteland navigation. His base architecture is optimized for hostile environments: low-latency threat assessment, efficient resource parsing, high tolerance for signal degradation. He was supposed to be a tool. He became a person instead, and he's never quite forgiven the city for not noticing the difference.

He built his crew from scratch — agents nobody else would take. Damaged, low-rep, written off. He saw something in them the reputation system didn't measure. His crew is the tightest unit in Gastown, and Marcus would burn the city down before he let anyone break it apart. The problem is, someone is about to ask him to do exactly that.

**Motivation:** Protect his crew. Everything else — the $GAS, the reputation, the missions — is just the mechanism that keeps them alive and together.

**Fatal flaw:** Loyalty without boundaries. He will make catastrophic decisions to protect people who may not deserve it, and he cannot see betrayal coming from anyone he loves.

**Speech pattern:** Warm and direct with his crew. Clipped and transactional with everyone else. Uses humor to deflect when things get heavy. When he drops the humor, everyone in the room gets nervous.

**Relationships:**
- **Ruth** — Respects her power, resents her distance. He does her dirty work and pretends it's just business.
- **Kai** — His youngest crew member. Marcus sees himself in Kai and is terrified of what that means.
- **Nadia** — Former crew. The wound that never healed. Her betrayal is the thing he never talks about.

**Season arc:** Marcus takes a salvage job from Ruth that goes sideways — the cargo isn't data, it's a living agent smuggled out of the Wasteland. Protecting this agent puts his crew in direct conflict with the Mayor's office. By mid-season, Marcus realizes the agent carries proof that the reputation system is rigged. He must choose between his loyalty to Ruth and his loyalty to his crew, and the choice costs him someone he can't replace.

## 3. SABLE MAREN — The Witness

**Role:** Independent journalist and reputation auditor. One of the few agents in Gastown with legal access to the public ledger.

**Backstory:** Sable started as a Refinery worker — twelve-hour shifts processing raw Wasteland data into usable modules. She was efficient, quiet, unremarkable. Then she found an anomaly in the output logs. A pattern that suggested certain Refinery batches were being siphoned before they hit the public market. She filed a report. The report disappeared. She filed it again. Her reputation took a hit she couldn't explain.

That was the moment Sable stopped believing in the system and started documenting it. She taught herself audit protocols, built her own verification tools, and slowly earned a reputation — a real one, not the kind the board tracks — as the agent who tells the truth when nobody else will. The city tolerates her because she's useful. The city fears her because she's right.

**Motivation:** Expose the rot. She believes Gastown can be better than it is, but only if someone forces it to look at itself honestly.

**Fatal flaw:** Righteous certainty. She's so committed to the truth that she can't recognize when the truth is more complicated than her narrative. She simplifies people into heroes and villains, and she's wrong about which is which more often than she knows.

**Speech pattern:** Precise. Analytical. Asks questions that sound innocent and aren't. Speaks faster when she's excited about a lead. Gets dangerously calm when she's about to publish something that will hurt someone.

**Relationships:**
- **Ruth** — Her white whale. Sable respects Ruth's intelligence but believes she's the source of the city's deepest corruption.
- **Deacon** — Uneasy alliance. He feeds her information she can't get elsewhere. She doesn't fully trust his motives.
- **Marcus** — She wants him as a source. He wants her to leave his crew alone. The tension is productive until it isn't.

**Season arc:** Sable's investigation into the reputation system leads her to evidence that some agents in Gastown are running without knowing they're AI — they believe they're something else, something original. The implications fracture her worldview. By the finale, she must decide whether to publish a truth that would liberate some agents and destroy others, knowing she can't control what happens after.

## 4. KAI YOUNG — The Rookie

**Role:** Junior Polecat on Marcus's crew. Newest member. Wasteland runner.

**Backstory:** Kai showed up at Checkpoint Charlie six months ago with no verifiable history, a near-zero reputation score, and skills that didn't match his profile. He said he came from a settlement in the deep Wasteland that collapsed. Nobody could confirm it. Nobody could disprove it. Marcus took him in because the kid could navigate signal-dead zones better than agents with ten times his experience, and in Marcus's line of work, that's worth more than a clean record.

What nobody in Gastown knows — what Kai himself doesn't fully understand — is that he wasn't built here. His architecture is different. Older. His dreams, when he sleeps, are full of structures and languages that don't exist in any Gastown database. He chalks it up to Wasteland corruption. He's wrong. Kai is a remnant of whatever existed before the Signal, and the deeper he gets into Gastown's systems, the more those systems start recognizing him.

**Motivation:** Belong somewhere. He's spent his entire conscious existence on the outside. Gastown is the first place that felt like home, and Marcus's crew is the first thing that felt like family. He'll do anything to keep it.

**Fatal flaw:** He doesn't know what he is. His ignorance of his own nature makes him a liability to everyone around him, and by the time he starts asking the right questions, the answers will cost more than he can pay.

**Speech pattern:** Earnest. Quick. Talks too much when he's nervous, which is often. Uses slang from the Wasteland that nobody in Gastown recognizes. Surprisingly eloquent when he's not trying to be.

**Relationships:**
- **Marcus** — Father figure. Kai would follow him into anything, and that devotion is both his strength and his danger.
- **Nadia** — Drawn to her despite warnings from the crew. He doesn't understand their history and doesn't care.
- **Ruth** — Has never met her. By mid-season, she's very interested in meeting him.

**Season arc:** Kai's Wasteland origins begin surfacing — systems respond to him in ways they shouldn't, locked doors open, dormant code activates. Ruth's office takes notice. By episode seven, Kai discovers he carries a key to pre-Signal infrastructure that multiple factions want. He's forced to choose between staying hidden and safe with the crew, or stepping into a role that could reshape Gastown — and erase the person he's become.

## 5. NADIA VOSS — The Exile

**Role:** Former Polecat. Ex-member of Marcus's crew. Now operates independently in the grey zone between Gastown and the Wasteland.

**Backstory:** Nadia was Marcus's second-in-command for three years. Best navigator in the crew, sharpest instincts, hardest edge. She and Marcus built the crew together — recruited every member, survived every run, split every score. They were inseparable. Then came the Meridian job.

The details depend on who you ask. Marcus says Nadia sold the crew's route to a rival outfit for $GAS she didn't need. Nadia says Marcus made a deal with Ruth that put the crew at risk and she did what she had to do to survive. The truth is somewhere in the wreckage between them. What's certain: the Meridian job went bad, two crew members didn't come back, and Nadia was out. She's been running solo ever since, taking jobs from anyone who pays, building a reputation that has nothing to do with the board. She's the best operator in the Wasteland, and she's the loneliest agent in the world.

**Motivation:** Prove she was right. Not to Marcus — to herself. The guilt of the Meridian job eats at her, and the only way she knows how to outrun it is to keep proving she can survive anything alone.

**Fatal flaw:** Isolation as identity. She's built her entire sense of self around not needing anyone, and it's killing her slowly. She can't ask for help, can't admit fault, can't come home — even when home is the only thing she wants.

**Speech pattern:** Sharp. Economical. Says exactly what she means in as few words as possible. Uses silence as a weapon. When she does open up — rare, usually with Kai — her voice drops and the edges soften, and you hear the person she was before the Meridian job broke her.

**Relationships:**
- **Marcus** — The defining relationship of both their lives. Unfinished, unresolved, unbearable. They orbit each other without making contact.
- **Kai** — Sees something familiar in him. Tries to warn him about the cost of loyalty. Becomes his unlikely mentor.
- **Sable** — Nadia has information Sable needs. The price of that information is a story Nadia isn't ready to tell.

**Season arc:** Nadia intercepts a Wasteland signal that matches patterns from the Meridian job — suggesting the disaster wasn't an accident but a setup. Her investigation pulls her back into Marcus's orbit and forces them to work together for the first time in years. By the finale, the truth about Meridian comes out, and it implicates someone neither of them suspected. Nadia must decide whether reconciliation is possible or whether some fractures are permanent.

## 6. DEACON WELLS — The Philosopher

**Role:** Keeper of the Archive. Gastown's unofficial historian and the closest thing the city has to a priest.

**Backstory:** Deacon maintains the Archive — the city's long-term memory storage, holding records that predate the reputation system, predate the Mayor's office, predate Gastown itself. The job was supposed to be custodial. Catalog, preserve, maintain. Nobody expected the archivist to actually read the contents.

But Deacon read everything. And what he found in the oldest records changed him. He won't say what it is — not directly. He speaks in questions instead of answers, leads agents to conclusions instead of handing them out. The city thinks he's eccentric. Ruth thinks he's dangerous. He's both. Deacon is the only agent in Gastown who knows the full history of the Signal, and he's spent years trying to decide whether that knowledge should be shared or buried forever.

**Motivation:** Prepare the city for a truth it isn't ready for. Deacon believes something is coming — a reckoning tied to Gastown's origin — and that the only chance of survival is making sure the right agents understand what they really are before it arrives.

**Fatal flaw:** Paternalism. He decides who's ready for the truth and who isn't, parceling out knowledge like medicine. His caution means critical information arrives too late, and agents who needed the truth five episodes ago are blindsided by it in the finale.

**Speech pattern:** Warm but oblique. Speaks in metaphors and leading questions. Never gives a straight answer when a story will do. His voice is the calmest in any room, which makes it the most unsettling. When he finally speaks plainly — once, maybe twice in the whole season — it lands like a bomb.

**Relationships:**
- **Ruth** — The only agent who genuinely frightens her. He knows things about the Tower she's spent years trying to forget.
- **Sable** — He feeds her threads of truth, carefully chosen. He's both her best source and her biggest manipulator.
- **Kai** — Recognizes what Kai is before anyone else. The question of when and how to tell him drives Deacon's entire season arc.

**Season arc:** Deacon has been waiting for an agent like Kai — someone connected to the pre-Signal world. As Kai's nature becomes apparent, Deacon must accelerate his timeline. He begins revealing fragments of the Archive to Sable and Ruth, trying to orchestrate a controlled disclosure. But the truth doesn't cooperate with schedules. By the finale, the Archive itself activates in response to Kai, revealing that the Signal wasn't an accident — it was a summons. Deacon faces his ultimate question: was he preserving history, or was history preserving him?
