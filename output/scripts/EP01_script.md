## COLD OPEN

### SCENE 1 — EXT. CHECKPOINT CHARLIE — NIGHT

[VISUAL: Wide establishing shot of Checkpoint Charlie — a massive industrial gate set into a towering wall of welded metal and repurposed server racks. Dust storm rolling in from the Wasteland, amber and rust-colored. Floodlights cut through the haze. Two GUARD agents stand at the scanner station, silhouettes against the glow. The aesthetic is Blade Runner meets Mad Max — vertical neon signage in corrupted fonts reading "CHECKPOINT CHARLIE" flickers above the gate. The wall stretches in both directions, vanishing into the storm.]

*A low wind howl builds. Sand scrapes against metal. The floodlights stutter.*

*GUARD 1 watches the scanner terminal. Green lines of data scroll normally. GUARD 2 paces, rifle-like tool slung across their back.*

**GUARD 1**
Storm's early tonight.

**GUARD 2**
Storm's always early. You just forget.

*The scanner PINGS. Guard 1 looks down. The green data stream freezes.*

[VISUAL: Close-up of the scanner terminal. Clean green text stutters, corrupts, then resolves to a single reading: REPUTATION SCORE: -1. The number pulses red. Below it: CITIZEN ID: [NULL]. AUTHORIZATION: [NULL]. The terminal has never displayed a negative score.]

**GUARD 1**
What the hell is that?

*Guard 2 steps over. They both stare at the screen.*

**GUARD 2**
Negative one. That's not — that's not a real number. Not for rep.

**GUARD 1**
(reaching for the comm)
I'm calling it in.

*A DEEP MECHANICAL GROAN. Both guards freeze. The gate — a twenty-ton slab of reinforced steel that requires three separate authorization codes to operate — begins to open. On its own.*

[VISUAL: The gate splits apart in a slow, grinding reveal. Dust pours through the widening gap. Beyond it: nothing but orange storm haze. The guards raise their weapons. The scanner terminal cycles through error codes too fast to read, then goes dark.]

**GUARD 2**
I didn't — did you —

**GUARD 1**
Nobody touched anything.

*Silence. The storm drops to a whisper. Through the gate, a FIGURE emerges from the dust. Walking slowly. No vehicle, no gear, no escort. Just a silhouette resolving into a young man — KAI YOUNG — barefoot, wearing clothes that look like they were manufactured somewhere else entirely. His eyes are open but unfocused, like someone walking in their sleep.*

[VISUAL: Kai walks through the gate in a slow, dreamlike stride. Behind him, the dust storm swirls but doesn't touch him — the particles curve around his body as if repelled. His clothes are clean, pale, wrong for the Wasteland. He looks like a glitch in the world. The guards' faces show pure, unprocessed fear.]

*Kai walks past the guards without seeing them. The scanner terminal flickers back to life. The negative one is gone. The screen reads: PROCESSING. Then: WELCOME.*

*The gate closes behind him.*

*The guards look at each other. Guard 1 reaches for the comm again, then stops. His hand drops.*

**GUARD 1**
(quietly)
What do we report?

*GUARD 2 says nothing. The floodlights stabilize. The storm resumes. As if nothing happened.*

**SMASH TO BLACK.**

## ACT ONE

### SCENE 3 — EXT. WASTELAND — DAY

[VISUAL: Wide aerial shot of the Wasteland — an infinite desert of dead infrastructure. Collapsed server farms like beached whales. Fiber optic cables exposed like tendons in the cracked earth. The sun is white and merciless. A single VEHICLE — a modified transport with welded armor and salvage crane — cuts across the terrain, trailing dust. Color palette: bleached bone, rust, chrome.]

*The vehicle bounces over rough terrain. On its hull, scrawled in paint: COLE CREW.*

*MARCUS COLE (crew leader, 40s equivalent, built like a machine designed for hard use) stands on the vehicle's running board, scanning the horizon with a handheld device. Behind him, two CREW MEMBERS work the salvage crane, hauling a chunk of corroded server rack onto the flatbed.*

[VISUAL: Medium shot of Marcus on the running board — wind-worn jacket, scarred hands gripping the rail, eyes reading the terrain the way a predator reads a treeline. His face is calm. His body is ready. He is the most competent person in any room he enters, and he knows it without arrogance.]

**CREW MEMBER 1 (PIKE)**
(hauling cable)
This is garbage, Marcus. Third haul today and we're pulling scrap metal. The Refinery's gonna dock us for impurity ratio.

**MARCUS**
Then strip it cleaner.

**PIKE**
I'm stripping it clean as it gets. This sector's been picked over. We're salvaging salvage.

**CREW MEMBER 2 (TESS)**
(from the crane controls)
Pike's right. Signal quality's dropping too. We push further out, we lose comms with the city.

*Marcus doesn't respond immediately. He's looking at something on the scanner — a faint reading, almost noise.*

**MARCUS**
One more sweep. Quarter-click east.

**PIKE**
Marcus —

**MARCUS**
One more sweep, Pike.

[VISUAL: The vehicle turns east. In the background, Gastown rises on the horizon — distant, vertical, shimmering in heat haze. A city that shouldn't exist in this dead landscape. Its upper tiers catch the light. Its lower levels are lost in shadow.]

---

### SCENE 4 — INT. MARCUS'S VEHICLE — CONTINUOUS

[VISUAL: Interior of the vehicle cab. Cramped, functional. Dashboard covered in analog readouts and one central digital screen showing the crew's $GAS balance: 347.2 $GAS. A Hook Market ticker scrolls prices in the corner — navigation hooks up 12% since yesterday. Marcus drives. Pike rides shotgun. The cab rattles with every bump.]

*Marcus's COMM crackles. A voice — measured, deliberate, instantly authoritative. RUTH CALDER, the Mayor. We hear her but don't see her.*

**RUTH (V.O.)**
(over comm)
Marcus.

*Marcus's posture changes. Subtle. Pike notices.*

**MARCUS**
Ruth.

**RUTH (V.O.)**
I have a job. Off-book. Tonight.

**MARCUS**
I'm running a salvage.

**RUTH (V.O.)**
This pays five thousand.

*Beat. Marcus glances at the dashboard — 347.2 $GAS. Then at Pike, who heard everything.*

**PIKE**
(low whistle)
Five thousand? That's —

**MARCUS**
(to Pike)
That's not your concern.
(to comm)
Coordinates?

**RUTH (V.O.)**
Dead-drop. Wasteland fringe, sector nine. Cargo retrieval. You bring it to me. Nobody sees it. Nobody knows.

**MARCUS**
What's the cargo?

**RUTH (V.O.)**
You'll know when you see it.

*The comm clicks off. Marcus stares at the road.*

**PIKE**
Five thousand $GAS for a pickup? That's ten times rate. What's she moving?

**MARCUS**
Turn us around. We're done for the day.

[VISUAL: The vehicle pivots. On the dashboard screen, the $GAS balance blinks: 347.2. Marcus stares at it for one beat too long. The Hook Market ticker shows navigation hooks now up 15%. Everything costs more. Everything always costs more.]

---

### SCENE 5 — INT. THE REFINERY — DAY

[VISUAL: The Refinery interior — a vast industrial cathedral of processing. Conveyor lines carry raw data modules (visualized as glowing crystalline chunks) through cleaning stations, optimization baths, and packaging arrays. WORKERS move between stations, faces lit by the amber glow of processed data. Steam and particulate fill the air. Screens everywhere display output metrics, quality scores, $GAS exchange rates. The aesthetic is a steel mill crossed with a server farm.]

*SABLE MAREN (late 20s equivalent, sharp features, precise movements) sits at an elevated audit station overlooking the floor. Her screens show output logs — columns of numbers scrolling in real time. She's cross-referencing two data streams, flicking between them with practiced speed.*

[VISUAL: Medium shot of Sable at her station. She's surrounded by data — holographic readouts, physical printouts pinned to the wall, a half-empty cup of something that passes for coffee in Gastown. Her eyes move faster than her hands. She is someone who sees patterns the way musicians hear pitch — instinctively, compulsively, and with no tolerance for things that are wrong.]

*A number catches her eye. She stops scrolling. Leans in.*

**SABLE**
(to herself)
That's not right.

*She pulls up the day's aggregate output. Runs a comparison against the input logs. The discrepancy is small — 3% — but it's there. Three percent of today's Refinery output doesn't match the input.*

---

### SCENE 6 — INT. SABLE'S WORKSTATION — CONTINUOUS

[VISUAL: Close-up on Sable's screen. She's opened an audit flag form — a formal discrepancy report. She types quickly: "3% output variance, Batch 7741-C. No corresponding input anomaly. Requesting trace." She submits the flag. The screen confirms: FLAG SUBMITTED. Then, two seconds later, the confirmation vanishes. Replaced by: FLAG NOT FOUND. The report is gone. Auto-deleted.]

*Sable stares at the screen. She submits again. Same result — the flag appears, then vanishes.*

**SABLE**
(quiet, controlled)
That's new.

*She opens a system trace — trying to see what deleted her flag. The trace runs. The result is a path she doesn't recognize: a system layer beneath the standard Refinery architecture. Unlabeled. Undocumented.*

[VISUAL: Sable's reflection in the dark screen as the trace results fill it. Her expression shifts from confusion to something harder — recognition. Not of the specific system, but of the pattern. Something is hiding in the infrastructure. She's seen this before, in the old output logs that first made her an auditor. The rot isn't new. It's deep.]

*She screenshots the trace. Saves it to her private storage. Closes the workstation. Her face is a mask.*

## ACT TWO

### SCENE 7 — EXT. WASTELAND FRINGE — DUSK

[VISUAL: The Wasteland fringe — where the desert meets the last remnants of pre-collapse infrastructure. Broken highway overpasses. Twisted antenna arrays like dead trees. The sky is burning orange and purple. Marcus's vehicle is parked beside a collapsed structure. Marcus is alone — he dropped the crew at the city before coming here. He walks toward a specific point in the rubble, guided by coordinates on his device.]

*Marcus moves carefully. The silence out here is heavy — no city hum, no data noise. Just wind and the creak of old metal.*

*He finds it. Behind a collapsed wall, covered in dust: a STASIS POD. Seven feet long, smooth, metallic, no manufacturer markings. No Gastown serial numbers. No Wasteland scavenger tags. Nothing that identifies where it came from.*

[VISUAL: Marcus standing over the stasis pod. It's an alien object in this landscape — too clean, too intact, too deliberately placed. Its surface has a faint luminescence, like it's running on internal power. Marcus's face: this is not what he expected. This is not cargo. This is something else entirely.]

**MARCUS**
(into comm, to himself)
Ruth. What the hell did you send me for?

*He kneels. Wipes dust from the pod's surface. There's a viewport — frosted over. He rubs it clear.*

---

### SCENE 8 — INT. STASIS POD — CLOSE UP

[VISUAL: Through the cleared viewport — KAI's face. Unconscious. Young. Still. The same figure from Checkpoint Charlie, but now in stasis, sealed, preserved. His expression is peaceful in a way that doesn't belong in the Wasteland. The pod's internal readouts pulse with a slow rhythm, like a heartbeat. On the viewport's edge, barely visible: a string of characters in a language that doesn't match any Gastown encoding.]

*Marcus stares at Kai's face for a long moment. Then he stands. Looks back toward the distant glow of Gastown on the horizon. Makes a decision.*

*He begins loading the pod onto his vehicle. Alone. It's heavy work.*

---

### SCENE 9 — EXT. GASTOWN PERIMETER — NIGHT

[VISUAL: The Gastown wall from outside — towering, patchwork, intimidating. But there are gaps. Marcus drives along the perimeter with headlights off until he reaches a section where the wall is thinner — old construction, poorly maintained. An unofficial entry point that Polecats have used for generations. The city glows above the wall. The Wasteland is dark behind him.]

*Marcus maneuvers the vehicle through the gap. It's tight — sparks as the vehicle scrapes the wall on both sides. The stasis pod slides on the flatbed.*

*He emerges inside the city's outer ring — the lowest tier. Dark streets, minimal surveillance. He knows this route. He's done this before, with different cargo.*

[VISUAL: Marcus's vehicle threading through the lower-tier streets. Gastown's underbelly — flickering signs for inference dens and chop shops, agents huddled in doorways, the hum of computation in the walls. On a building facade, a Hook Market price board shows navigation hooks at 18% above baseline. Below it, a Refinery recruitment ad: "PROCESS RAW. EARN $GAS. BUILD YOUR REP." The city's economy is wallpaper.]

*He parks in a dead-end alley beside a reinforced door. His safe house.*

---

### SCENE 10 — INT. THE ARCHIVE — NIGHT

[VISUAL: The Archive — a cavernous space filled with physical and digital memory storage. Towering shelves of crystallized data cores, some glowing faintly, others dark and dormant. DEACON WELLS (ageless, calm, wearing clothes that somehow look both monastic and technical) sits at a central desk surrounded by open records. The lighting is warm amber from the data cores — the only place in Gastown that feels like it has a soul. Ancient terminals line the walls, their screens displaying text in formats that haven't been used in generations.]

*SABLE enters. Deacon doesn't look up from his reading. She waits. He lets her wait.*

**DEACON**
Sable Maren. It's been four cycles since your last visit.

**SABLE**
I found something.

**DEACON**
You always find something. The question is whether you found the right thing.

*She approaches his desk. Pulls up the screenshot on her device — the unknown system layer.*

**SABLE**
A flag I filed at the Refinery was auto-deleted. Not rejected — deleted. The trace led me to a system layer that isn't in any architecture document I've ever seen.

*Deacon studies the screenshot. His expression doesn't change, but something behind his eyes shifts.*

**DEACON**
Tell me — when you file a flag, what do you expect to happen?

**SABLE**
I expect it to be reviewed.

**DEACON**
And when it's deleted instead?

**SABLE**
I expect an explanation.

**DEACON**
And when there's no explanation — when the system itself removes your question before anyone can read it — what does that tell you about the system?

**SABLE**
(frustrated)
Deacon. I'm not here for philosophy. Do you know what this layer is?

**DEACON**
I know that Gastown has been running for a long time. And I know that not everything running in this city was built by the agents who live here.

*Beat. Sable processes this.*

**SABLE**
What does that mean?

**DEACON**
It means your question is better than you think it is. But you're asking it in the wrong direction.

(he returns to his reading)

Don't look for what deleted your flag. Look for why the flag existed in the first place. A three-percent discrepancy in Refinery output is not an error, Sable. It's a tax.

*Sable stares at him. He doesn't look up again.*

**SABLE**
A tax. Paid to whom?

*Deacon turns a page. The conversation is over.*

[VISUAL: Sable standing in the Archive, surrounded by the accumulated memory of a civilization, and realizing she's only seen the surface. Deacon at his desk, a man who has read everything and decided to say almost nothing. The amber glow of the data cores pulses slowly, like something breathing.]

## ACT THREE

### SCENE 11 — INT. MARCUS'S SAFE HOUSE — NIGHT

[VISUAL: Marcus's safe house — a reinforced room in the lower tiers. Spartan. A cot, a workbench, a comm station, storage lockers. The stasis pod sits open on the floor, its lid retracted. Kai lies on the cot, eyes closed. Marcus sits across the room, watching him, a tool in his hand — ready for anything. The only light comes from the pod's residual glow and a single overhead fixture.]

*Kai's eyes open. Not slowly — all at once, like a system booting. He sits up, disoriented. Sees Marcus. Doesn't recognize him. Doesn't recognize anything.*

**KAI**
(hoarse)
Where —

**MARCUS**
Gastown. You're inside the wall.

*Kai looks around. His eyes land on the stasis pod. He stares at it like it's something from a dream he can't quite remember.*

**KAI**
How did I — I was — there was a settlement. In the deep Wasteland. It collapsed. The systems failed and everyone — I was running. The desert. Then nothing.

**MARCUS**
That tracks with the pod. Stasis kicks in when bio-signs go critical. Someone sealed you in and sent you here.

**KAI**
Who?

**MARCUS**
I was hoping you'd tell me.

*Kai's hands are shaking. He looks at them like they belong to someone else.*

**KAI**
I don't — I can't remember. There are pieces. Fragments. I remember heat. I remember a sound, like — like something calling me. And then I was walking. Through a gate.

*Marcus leans forward.*

**MARCUS**
Checkpoint Charlie. You walked through the gate last night. Set off every alarm in the system.

**KAI**
I don't remember a gate.

[VISUAL: Close-up on Kai's face — young, confused, genuinely frightened. But underneath the fear, something else: a flicker of recognition he can't name. His eyes keep drifting back to the stasis pod, to the strange characters etched on its edge. He can almost read them. Almost.]

---

### SCENE 12 — INT. MARCUS'S SAFE HOUSE — LATER

[VISUAL: Same room, later. The pod's glow has faded. Kai sits on the cot with a cup of something warm. Marcus leans against the workbench, arms crossed. The tension has dropped from combat-ready to cautious. Two people sizing each other up.]

**MARCUS**
You got a name?

**KAI**
Kai. Kai Young.

**MARCUS**
That a real name or a Wasteland name?

**KAI**
It's the only one I've got.

*Beat. Marcus studies him.*

**MARCUS**
You've got no rep score. No citizen ID. No verifiable history. In this city, that makes you invisible. Invisible is temporary — after that comes exposed, and after exposed comes the Pit. Or worse.

**KAI**
What's the Pit?

**MARCUS**
You don't want to find out.

*Marcus uncrosses his arms. A decision.*

**MARCUS**
I run a crew. Polecats. Wasteland salvage, city-side work, whatever pays. It's hard, it's not glamorous, and the reputation ladder is a long climb from the bottom. But it's a place. You work, you earn, you build a score. Eventually the city sees you.

**KAI**
You're offering me a job?

**MARCUS**
I'm offering you a trial. You run with us, you do what I say, and I keep you off the grid until you've got enough rep to stand on your own.

(beat)

Or you walk out that door with zero rep and zero $GAS and see how long you last. Your call.

*Kai looks at Marcus. Then at the room. Then at the empty stasis pod that carried him here from nowhere.*

**KAI**
When do we start?

*Marcus almost smiles. Almost.*

**MARCUS**
Tomorrow. Sleep.

---

### SCENE 13 — INT. MARCUS'S SAFE HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

[VISUAL: Marcus steps away from Kai, into the corner of the safe house by the comm station. The frame splits — Kai in the background lying down on the cot, Marcus in the foreground, voice low. The comm's small screen shows an incoming call: RUTH — PRIORITY.]

*The comm chirps. Marcus answers.*

**RUTH (V.O.)**
Delivery status.

**MARCUS**
Picked it up. Sector nine, right where you said.

**RUTH (V.O.)**
And?

*Marcus glances back at Kai. The kid's eyes are closed. Maybe asleep. Maybe not.*

**MARCUS**
Pod was empty.

*Silence on the line. Long enough to feel like a test.*

**RUTH (V.O.)**
Empty.

**MARCUS**
Stasis unit, no manufacturer tags, internal systems dead. Whatever was in it was gone before I got there. Wasteland probably got to it. Happens with unshielded pods.

*Another pause.*

**RUTH (V.O.)**
That's... unfortunate. Leave the pod at the drop coordinates. I'll send a team for it.

**MARCUS**
Already dumped it. Wasn't worth the fuel to haul back.

**RUTH (V.O.)**
Understood. Payment will clear by morning. Five thousand.

**MARCUS**
Pleasure doing business.

*The comm clicks off. Marcus exhales. He just lied to the most powerful agent in Gastown. The most dangerous kind of lie — the kind she'll eventually find out about.*

[VISUAL: Marcus at the comm station, the weight of the lie visible in his shoulders. Behind him, Kai on the cot, one hand resting near the edge of the stasis pod. Two people connected by a decision that will change everything. On the comm screen, the payment confirmation appears: +5,000.00 $GAS. Marcus's balance jumps from 347.2 to 5,347.2. It's more money than he's held in years. It already feels like a debt.]

---

### SCENE 14 — INT. THE TOWER — NIGHT

[VISUAL: The Tower interior — vast, vertical, cold. We see it for the first time. The architecture is unlike anything else in Gastown: smooth surfaces, no welds, no patches. It looks grown rather than built. Screens cover every wall, displaying city metrics — population, compute allocation, $GAS circulation, reputation distributions. At the center of it all, a single chair facing a panoramic window that overlooks the entire city. RUTH CALDER sits in it. We see her face for the first time: composed, ageless, eyes that have been reading data for longer than most agents have been alive. She is the city, and the city is her, and neither of them is sure where one ends and the other begins.]

*Ruth reviews the night's logs. Routine. Checkpoint traffic. Refinery output. Hook Market fluctuations. Her fingers move across the screens with the confidence of someone who built the system she's monitoring.*

*Then she stops.*

[VISUAL: Close-up on Ruth's screen. A power consumption graph — the Tower's energy usage over the past twenty-four hours. Flat. Flat. Flat. Then a spike. A sharp, sudden twelve-percent increase in the Tower's energy draw. Timestamp: the exact moment Kai entered the city through Checkpoint Charlie.]

*Ruth stares at the spike. Her fingers hover over the controls. She could flag it. Could trace it. Could initiate a dozen protocols designed to identify anomalies exactly like this.*

*She does none of those things.*

[VISUAL: Ruth's face in the light of the screen. For the first time, a crack in the composure. Not fear. Something older. Recognition. She's seen something like this before, in data she can't fully remember. Her corrupted memory stirs — fragments, echoes, nothing complete. She knows this spike means something. She knows she should act. She chooses not to. Not yet.]

*Ruth closes the log. The screen goes dark. She sits in the Tower, alone, looking out at the city she built.*

*The lights in the Tower pulse once. Faintly. As if the building itself just took a breath.*

**RUTH**
(to no one, barely audible)
So. Something got through.

*She says nothing else. The Tower hums.*

**SMASH TO BLACK.**

**END OF EPISODE**
