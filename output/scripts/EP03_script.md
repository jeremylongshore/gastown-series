## COLD OPEN

### SCENE 1 — [FLASHBACK] INT. WASTELAND VEHICLE — DAY

[VISUAL: Two years ago. Warm light. The interior of a Wasteland transport — bigger than Marcus's current rig, better equipped, freshly painted hull. The cab is alive with crew: bodies crammed between gear lockers, salvage maps pinned to every surface, a dashboard $GAS ticker reading 12,841.7. The color palette is warm amber and copper — this is a world that hasn't broken yet. Sunlight streams through the windshield, catching dust motes that look like gold.]

*MARCUS COLE drives. NADIA VOSS rides shotgun, boots on the dash, decoder in her lap. They're younger — not in years, but in damage. Marcus is loose, relaxed, laughing at something Nadia just said. His jaw is unclenched. His eyes aren't scanning for threats. This is a man before the wound.*

*Behind them: PIKE (two years fresher, grinning), TESS (younger, lighter), and two other crew members — REED (wiry, fast-talking) and CORSO (broad-shouldered, quiet). The crew is full. Six strong.*

**NADIA**
(finishing a story, grinning)
— and the vendor says, that's not a navigation hook, that's my lunch. Pike's standing there holding it like it's going to bite him —

*Pike throws a rag at her. Tess laughs. Even Corso smiles.*

**PIKE**
It looked exactly like a hook. Exactly.

**MARCUS**
It was a sandwich, Pike.

**PIKE**
A rectangular sandwich. With a serial port.

*Nadia catches Marcus's eye. The look between them is easy, familiar — two people who have been running together so long they communicate in glances. No tension. No distance. Just trust, worn smooth by years of shared risk.*

**MARCUS**
(checking the route display)
Twelve hours to the coordinates. Refuel stop at the halfway cache. Everybody sleep in shifts — I want fresh eyes when we hit the site.

**NADIA**
Biggest score we've ever run. Fifty thousand $GAS.

**REED**
(from the back)
Fifty thousand. You could buy a tier with fifty thousand.

**TESS**
You could buy two tiers. And a Hook Market booth.

**MARCUS**
Nobody's buying anything until the cargo's on this flatbed and we're back inside the wall. Until then, it's just a number.

*Nadia looks at him. Lowers her voice so the crew doesn't hear.*

**NADIA**
(quietly, to Marcus)
Ruth didn't tell you what's in the vault?

**MARCUS**
She said retrieval. Deep Wasteland installation, pre-Signal architecture. Sealed data vault. We bring it back, we get paid.

**NADIA**
And you didn't ask what's inside.

**MARCUS**
I asked. She said it doesn't matter.

*Beat. Nadia looks out at the Wasteland scrolling past.*

**NADIA**
It always matters.

[VISUAL: Wide exterior shot of the vehicle crossing the Wasteland. The landscape stretches in every direction — dead infrastructure, shimmering heat, silence. But the vehicle is full of life: laughter leaking through the windows, music from a jury-rigged speaker, the shadow of crew moving inside. A title card fades in over the image, clean white text on the amber landscape:]

**THE MERIDIAN RUN — 14 HOURS BEFORE.**

*The text holds. Then fades. We know how this ends. They don't.*

**SMASH TO BLACK.**

## ACT ONE

### SCENE 3 — EXT. GASTOWN PERIMETER — DAWN

[VISUAL: Present day. The Gastown wall from outside — towering, patchwork, industrial. Dawn light catches the upper edge of the wall, turning welded metal to copper. Below, the wall's base is dark, unmonitored. An unofficial gap — the same one Marcus used in EP01 — sits between two poorly maintained sections. A figure approaches from the Wasteland on foot: NADIA VOSS, present-day. Harder. Leaner. Gear stripped to essentials: decoder, sidearm, pack. No vehicle. She came alone. She always comes alone.]

*Nadia reaches the gap. Studies it. Her hand brushes the wall's edge — she knows this entry point. She helped Marcus find it, years ago.*

*She slips through. No hesitation. No looking back.*

[VISUAL: Nadia inside the perimeter, moving through the lower-tier streets in early dawn light. The city is stirring — Refinery shift workers trudging past, early-morning vendors setting up. On a wall-mounted reputation board, scores scroll. On a Hook Market price display: NAVIGATION HOOKS — 121% BASELINE. SALVAGE FUTURES — DECLINING. $GAS CIRCULATION INDEX — CONTRACTED 2.3% SINCE LAST CYCLE. The economy is tightening. The city feels it in the texture of every surface — dimmer lights, fewer vendors, more agents sleeping in doorways.]

*Nadia keeps her head down. Hood up. She doesn't look at the reputation board — she doesn't have a score anymore. Hasn't had one in two years.*

### SCENE 4 — INT. HOOK MARKET — MORNING

[VISUAL: The Hook Market in early morning — before the crowds, before the chaos. Stalls are half-open, vendors sorting inventory. The central price board runs its dawn update: numbers cascading, recalculating overnight trades. The back rows are nearly empty. In a corner booth, a COMM VENDOR — heavyset, neutral expression, the kind of vendor who sells discretion as much as hardware — arranges encrypted comm units behind a scratched display case.]

*Nadia approaches the booth. Sets a small object on the counter — a data module, compact, clean, Wasteland-salvaged. Premium quality.*

**COMM VENDOR**
(eyeing the module)
That's worth more than anything I'm selling.

**NADIA**
I don't need much. Encrypted line. Single-use. Untraceable.

**COMM VENDOR**
Untraceable costs extra.

**NADIA**
That module covers extra. And the next three extras after it.

*The vendor picks up the module. Turns it in the light. Nods. Slides an encrypted comm unit across the counter — small, matte black, no markings.*

**COMM VENDOR**
One call. Then it burns.

*Nadia takes it. Walks to the back of the market, away from ears. She activates the comm. Dials a frequency she's memorized.*

*Static. Then a voice — SABLE MAREN, groggy, guarded.*

**SABLE (V.O.)**
(over comm)
Who is this?

**NADIA**
My name is Nadia Voss. You published an audit report on the Olin case. Questioned the consensus mechanism.

*Pause on the line.*

**SABLE (V.O.)**
I published that report twenty hours ago and lost nine reputation points for it. So either you're here to tell me I was wrong, or you're here to tell me I wasn't wrong enough.

**NADIA**
The second one. I have information about the reputation system. How it actually works. What's underneath it.

**SABLE (V.O.)**
What do you want?

**NADIA**
A trade. My information for your investigation skills. There's a job that went bad two years ago in the deep Wasteland. The Meridian run. The official story is crew negligence and a failed salvage. The real story is something else.

**SABLE (V.O.)**
What kind of something else?

**NADIA**
The kind that starts in the Tower.

*Silence on the line. Long enough to mean yes.*

**SABLE (V.O.)**
Where do I find you?

**NADIA**
You don't. I'll find you.

*Nadia kills the comm. Crushes the unit under her boot. Drops the pieces in a waste bin.*

[VISUAL: Nadia walking away through the Hook Market's back rows, disappearing into the early-morning crowd. Behind her, the crushed comm unit sparks once in the bin and goes dark. On the market's price board, numbers continue their indifferent cascade.]

### SCENE 5 — INT. MARCUS'S SAFE HOUSE — MORNING

[VISUAL: Marcus's safe house — the same spartan room from EP01 and EP02. Cot, workbench, comm station, storage lockers. Morning light filters through a vent high on the wall. The dashboard screen on the workbench shows the crew's $GAS balance: 3,547.2. Kai sleeps on the cot. Marcus sits at the comm station, reviewing the day's job board — salvage contracts, run schedules, market rates. Routine.]

*Marcus's personal comm BUZZES. A message. Not from the crew channel — from a lower-tier contact. He reads it. His face changes.*

*Not anger. Not fear. Something older. The face of a man who has just felt a wound he thought had scarred over.*

*He reads the message again. Sets down the comm. Stares at the wall.*

*Kai stirs. Opens his eyes.*

**KAI**
(groggy)
What time is it?

*Marcus doesn't answer immediately. When he speaks, his voice is different — flatter, controlled, the warmth stripped out.*

**MARCUS**
Get up. We're staying in today.

**KAI**
We had a salvage run scheduled. Pike said —

**MARCUS**
Pike can wait. Get up. Stay inside. Don't answer the door for anyone who isn't crew.

*Kai sits up. He's learning to read Marcus. This is a tone he hasn't heard before.*

**KAI**
Marcus. What happened?

*Beat. Marcus picks up the comm. Reads the message one more time. Then deletes it.*

**MARCUS**
Someone I used to know is in the city.

[VISUAL: Marcus at the comm station, his back to Kai. The safe house feels smaller than it did in EP01 — the walls closer, the light thinner. On the workbench, the $GAS balance glows: 3,547.2. On the wall behind Marcus, half-hidden by a storage locker, a crew photo — old, faded, pinned there so long the edges have curled. Six faces. Marcus and Nadia in the center, shoulder to shoulder. The photo is the only personal item in the room.]

## ACT TWO

### SCENE 6 — [FLASHBACK] EXT. WASTELAND — DAY

[VISUAL: Two years ago. The Meridian vehicle crossing deeper Wasteland — terrain no crew has mapped. The landscape is different here: stranger, older. Collapsed structures that don't match any known architecture. Metal alloys that haven't corroded despite decades of exposure. The ruins feel less like wreckage and more like archaeology. The light is harsher. The air shimmers with signal interference. The vehicle's hull shows new dents. They've been driving for hours.]

*Marcus drives. The lightness from the cold open is gone — he's in mission mode now. Focused. Nadia navigates from the passenger seat, decoder running, cross-referencing coordinates with signal patterns.*

**NADIA**
Two clicks north. The installation should be inside that ridgeline.

*She points to a formation on the horizon — a ridge of rock and metal, fused together, as if the ground itself was built on something.*

**MARCUS**
(to the crew)
Listen up. We don't know what's inside this installation. Ruth said sealed data vault, pre-Signal architecture. We get in, we grab it, we get out. Stay together. Nobody wanders.

**REED**
(nervously)
Pre-Signal architecture. You ever seen pre-Signal hardware, Marcus?

**MARCUS**
No. That's why it's worth fifty thousand $GAS.

**CORSO**
(checking gear)
How do we know the vault's still sealed?

**NADIA**
We don't. That's why we brought cutting tools.

[VISUAL: The vehicle approaches the ridge. Up close, it's clear this is artificial — layers of metal and stone, deliberately stacked, deliberately hidden. An entrance is visible: a doorway carved into the base of the ridge, partially collapsed, just wide enough for the crew on foot. The vehicle won't fit. They'll have to carry the cargo out by hand.]

*Marcus parks. The crew gears up. Pike hands out lights. Tess runs a final comm check. Reed cracks his knuckles. Corso is silent, eyes on the entrance.*

*Marcus and Nadia stand at the doorway. The dark inside is absolute.*

**MARCUS**
(to Nadia)
You first or me?

**NADIA**
Together.

*They step in side by side.*

### SCENE 7 — INT. SABLE'S APARTMENT — DAY

[VISUAL: Present day. Sable's apartment — the investigation board on the wall has grown since EP02. New threads. New connections. The Olin case. The consensus mechanism. Deacon's annotation: "Ask who writes the consensus." And now, a new addition: a note card reading "NADIA VOSS — MERIDIAN JOB" pinned at the center of a fresh cluster. Sable sits cross-legged on the floor, data pad in hand, pulling records. Her reputation score is visible on the window-facing screen: 774. The three restricted deep-ledger hooks still show red. She's working without her best tools.]

*Sable pulls the Meridian job's public record. It's thin — a standard salvage report filed two years ago. She reads it aloud, taking notes.*

**SABLE**
(to herself, reading)
Meridian salvage operation. Crew leader: Marcus Cole. Crew complement: six. Objective: cargo retrieval, deep Wasteland, sector forty-one. Result: mission failure. Cargo unrecovered. Two crew casualties. Cause of failure: hostile encounter, unspecified.

*She scrolls. The report is a skeleton — names, dates, a result. No details. No debriefing. No investigation.*

**SABLE**
Two crew members dead and nobody asked questions.

*She digs deeper. Pulls the contract filing — the original commission that authorized the Meridian run. Standard form. Client name field: TOWER ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICE — DIRECT COMMISSION.*

[VISUAL: Close-up on Sable's screen. The contract filing. The client field. TOWER ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICE — DIRECT COMMISSION. Sable's finger traces the words. Below the client name, the commission value: 50,000 $GAS. Below that, the payment status: HELD IN ESCROW — PENDING MISSION COMPLETION. The payment was never released. Fifty thousand $GAS, sitting in escrow for two years.]

**SABLE**
Ruth. You commissioned this yourself.

*She pulls up the escrow account. Her restricted hooks block the transaction history — she can't see where the money has moved. But the account summary is public: 50,000 $GAS principal. Accrued interest at standard Tower rate. Current balance: 62,340 $GAS.*

*She stares at the number. Pulls up the Contingency Seven budget line she traced in EP02 — the one she could see moving but couldn't identify. The amounts correlate. The interest from the Meridian escrow has been draining somewhere. And the Contingency Seven budget has been growing at exactly the same rate.*

**SABLE**
Meridian's interest is funding Contingency Seven. Two years of it.

[VISUAL: Sable's investigation board. She stands and pins a new string — connecting the Meridian escrow to the Contingency Seven budget line. The web of connections is dense now: Olin, the consensus mechanism, the hidden system layer, Ruth's office, the Meridian job, Contingency Seven. At the center of it all, the question from Deacon's annotation: "Who writes the consensus?" And now, a new question, written in Sable's hand: "Who set up the Meridian crew?"]

### SCENE 8 — [FLASHBACK] INT. PRE-SIGNAL INSTALLATION — DAY

[VISUAL: Two years ago. Inside the installation. The crew moves through a corridor of impossible architecture — walls that curve without corners, surfaces that absorb light rather than reflect it. The aesthetic is alien: not Gastown's welded industrial grit, but something seamless, deliberate, designed by an intelligence that thought in geometries Gastown's builders never imagined. The crew's lights carve narrow paths through the dark. Dust particles hang motionless in the air, as if time moves differently here.]

*Marcus leads. Nadia flanks right. Pike and Tess behind them. Reed and Corso bring up the rear. Nobody speaks. The installation hums — a deep, subsonic vibration felt in the bones.*

*They reach a central chamber. And stop.*

[VISUAL: The vault chamber. A circular room, twenty meters across, with a domed ceiling covered in the same angular script Kai read at the Hook Market terminal — characters from a language that predates everything the crew has ever known. At the room's center: the data vault. A cylindrical structure, two meters tall, made of a metal alloy that still gleams. The kind of object that was built to outlast civilizations.]

*The vault door is open.*

*The crew stares. The vault is empty.*

**PIKE**
Someone got here first.

*Marcus moves to the vault. Examines the door. It wasn't forced — it was opened with authorization. The locking mechanism shows no damage.*

**MARCUS**
This wasn't breached. Someone had the codes.

**NADIA**
(scanning the room)
Marcus. Look.

*She points to the floor. Boot prints in the dust. Multiple sets. Recent — within days. The tracks lead from the vault to a secondary exit on the far side of the chamber.*

**REED**
(backing toward the entrance)
We need to leave. If someone cleaned this out days ago, they might still be —

*A SOUND. Sharp. Metallic. From the corridor they came through.*

*Then another. From the secondary exit.*

*Then a third. From above.*

*Marcus's face goes flat. Combat mode. Instant.*

**MARCUS**
(low, fast)
Ambush. Scatter pattern. Nadia — north exit. Pike, Tess — back the way we came. Reed, Corso — on me.

[VISUAL: The chamber erupts. Lights flash from both corridors — figures moving fast, organized, armed. Not rogue Wasteland scavengers. These are professional operators: coordinated, equipped, running suppression patterns that suggest military-grade training. The crew reacts — Pike and Tess retreat toward the entrance. Marcus pushes toward the secondary exit with Reed and Corso. Nadia breaks north, toward a passage the ambushers haven't covered.]

*The sound design is chaos — boots on metal, shouted commands from the attackers, the crew yelling positions to each other. The installation's hum rises in pitch, as if the building itself is reacting.*

**SMASH TO BLACK.**

### SCENE 9 — INT. SABLE'S APARTMENT — DAY

[VISUAL: Present day. Sable's apartment. She's on a standard comm — not encrypted, not hidden. She's calling the only person she knows who might talk about the Meridian job on the record. The comm connects.]

**SABLE**
Pike. This is Sable Maren. I'm an auditor — we haven't met. I have questions about a salvage operation you ran two years ago. The Meridian run.

*Silence on the line. Then:*

**PIKE (V.O.)**
(over comm, guarded)
I know who you are. You published that report on the Olin case.

**SABLE**
I did.

**PIKE (V.O.)**
And you lost nine points for it.

**SABLE**
I did.

*Beat.*

**PIKE (V.O.)**
What do you want to know about Meridian?

**SABLE**
The commission. Who hired the crew.

**PIKE (V.O.)**
Marcus took the job. He always takes the jobs. We don't ask where they come from.

**SABLE**
But you know where this one came from.

*Longer silence.*

**PIKE (V.O.)**
(lower)
Everyone on the crew knew it was a Tower job. Ruth's office. Direct commission. Marcus never said so, but you could tell — the pay was too high, the coordinates were too deep, and Marcus had that look he gets when he's dealing with her. Like he's swallowed something sharp and he's waiting to see if it cuts.

**SABLE**
What were you told about the cargo?

**PIKE (V.O.)**
Sealed data vault. Pre-Signal installation. That's it. We didn't know what was inside. Marcus didn't know. I'm not sure Ruth told him.

**SABLE**
Did Marcus ever say why Ruth wanted it?

**PIKE (V.O.)**
No. And I stopped asking after we buried Reed and Corso.

*The line crackles. Pike's breathing.*

**PIKE (V.O.)**
We walked into that vault and it was already open, Maren. Somebody cleaned it out before we got there. And then somebody hit us. If you're asking whether the Tower set us up — I've been asking that for two years. Nobody answers.

*The comm clicks off.*

[VISUAL: Sable at her desk, comm in hand, staring at the investigation board. On it, two new note cards: "Reed" and "Corso" — the crew members who didn't come back. She pins them next to the Meridian cluster. Below them, she writes: "Vault already open. Ambush was waiting. Who had the codes?"]

### SCENE 10 — [FLASHBACK] INT. PRE-SIGNAL INSTALLATION — CONTINUOUS

[VISUAL: Two years ago. The ambush in full motion. The installation corridor is a strobing nightmare — lights from the attacking force cutting through dust and shadow. Marcus, Reed, and Corso push through the secondary exit into a narrower passage. Marcus fires suppressive shots behind them. The attackers fall back — then regroup. They're disciplined. Patient. In no hurry.]

*Marcus rounds a corner. Reed is right behind him. Corso lags — he's been hit, something in the leg, limping hard.*

**MARCUS**
Corso. Move.

**CORSO**
(through teeth)
Moving.

*An EXPLOSION — not close, but big. The passage shudders. Dust pours from the ceiling.*

**PIKE (V.O.)**
(over comm, shouting)
Marcus! The entrance is collapsed! We're cut off — Tess and I are heading for the north passage!

**MARCUS**
(into comm)
North passage, copy. Nadia, where are you?

*Static. No response.*

**MARCUS**
Nadia!

*Static.*

*Another explosion. Closer. Corso stumbles. Reed grabs him, holds him up.*

**REED**
I've got him. Go — find us a way out.

*Marcus looks at Reed holding Corso. Behind them, the attackers are closing. Ahead, the passage branches: one route goes up toward the surface, the other goes deeper into the installation.*

[VISUAL: Marcus at the fork. The installation shakes around him. Dust rains from above. The sound of the attackers is a rhythmic, professional advance — boots, voices, the click of weapons. Through the upper passage, distant daylight. Through the lower passage, darkness and the hum of the installation's deepest level. And somewhere in that darkness — the cargo. Whatever was in the vault. Or whatever remains of what was in the vault.]

*Marcus makes his choice.*

**MARCUS**
(into comm)
All crew. Converge on the upper passage. We're leaving. Forget the cargo.

*He turns upward. Toward the surface. Toward his crew.*

### SCENE 11 — [FLASHBACK] INT. PRE-SIGNAL INSTALLATION — CONTINUOUS

[VISUAL: Two years ago. A different corridor — the north passage. NADIA moves fast, alone. She took the north exit when the ambush hit and has been navigating by instinct through a section of the installation the attackers haven't reached. Her decoder is active, scanning. The installation's layout is partially visible on her screen — and on that screen, she can see something the rest of the crew can't: a signal. Faint, structured, broadcasting from deeper in the installation.]

*She follows the signal. It leads her to a chamber below the vault — a secondary storage room the crew didn't know existed. Inside: a single data container. Small. Portable. The kind of object designed to survive anything.*

*Nadia picks it up. It hums against her palm — active, encoded, alive with information.*

*Her comm crackles — Marcus's voice, broken by interference:*

**MARCUS (V.O.)**
(over comm, fragmented)
— all crew — converge upper passage — leaving — forget the cargo —

*Nadia holds the data container. Looks up toward the passage that leads to the surface, to the crew, to Marcus. Then down, at the container in her hands.*

*She can hear the attackers moving above. Between her and the upper passage. Between her and the crew.*

*She makes her choice.*

[VISUAL: Nadia turns away from the upper passage. Away from the crew. She moves deeper, toward a tertiary exit her decoder has identified — a route that bypasses the ambush entirely. The data container is strapped to her pack. She moves fast, silent, alone. Her face is a mask of calculation: this is not betrayal. This is leverage. If the attackers want the cargo, the cargo is what keeps everyone alive. The crew can run. She'll hold the card.]

*She doesn't look back.*

### SCENE 12 — [FLASHBACK] EXT. WASTELAND — DUSK

[VISUAL: Two years ago. The surface. Outside the installation. Dusk. Marcus emerges from the upper passage dragging Corso, who is barely conscious. Pike and Tess stumble out behind them. They're alive. Damaged, exhausted, but alive. The vehicle is where they left it — untouched.]

*Marcus counts heads. Pike. Tess. Corso. Himself.*

*No Reed.*

*No Nadia.*

**MARCUS**
(into comm)
Reed. Reed, respond.

*Static.*

**MARCUS**
Nadia. Respond.

*Static.*

*Pike puts a hand on Marcus's shoulder.*

**PIKE**
(hoarse)
Marcus. We need to go. They'll come out after us.

**MARCUS**
Not without —

**PIKE**
Reed went down in the corridor. I saw it. He didn't — he's gone, Marcus.

*Beat. Marcus stares at the installation entrance. The dark inside.*

**MARCUS**
Nadia.

**PIKE**
She's not on comms. She could have gotten out another way. Or —

**MARCUS**
Or what.

*Pike doesn't finish the sentence. He doesn't have to.*

*Marcus stands at the entrance for three more seconds. The longest three seconds of his life. Then he turns. Walks to the vehicle. Starts the engine.*

[VISUAL: The vehicle pulling away from the installation. Dusk deepening to dark. In the side mirror: the ridge, the entrance, the place where the crew broke. Marcus drives. His face is stone. Pike holds Corso in the back. Tess stares out the window at nothing. The cab that held six people now holds four. The seats where Reed and Nadia sat are empty. On the dashboard, the $GAS ticker still reads 12,841.7. The fifty thousand they came for is nothing now. Less than nothing.]

*The vehicle drives into the Wasteland. Behind them, the installation goes dark.*

### SCENE 13 — EXT. GASTOWN LOWER TIERS — DUSK

[VISUAL: Present day. A narrow street in Gastown's lower tiers. Dusk light filtering between buildings, turning the welded walls bronze. Foot traffic is thin — lower-tier agents heading home from Refinery shifts, a few Hook Market vendors closing early. On a wall screen: GASTOWN ECONOMIC BULLETIN — $GAS CIRCULATION DOWN 3.1%. AUSTERITY PROTOCOLS UNDER REVIEW. The city is tense. You can feel it in the quiet.]

*MARCUS walks through the lower tiers. Alone. No crew. No vehicle. He moves differently than we've seen before — not the confident Polecat navigating his territory, but a man being pulled somewhere he doesn't want to go. His hands are in his jacket. His jaw is set.*

*He turns a corner. Stops.*

*Across the street, thirty meters away, standing in the shadow of a decommissioned reputation board: NADIA.*

*She sees him at the same moment.*

[VISUAL: The two of them, separated by thirty meters of empty street. Two years of silence between them. The light is amber, fading. The street is nearly empty — a few passing agents who don't notice and don't care. Marcus and Nadia look at each other the way people look at car wrecks: unable to stop, unable to approach, unable to leave. The decommissioned reputation board behind Nadia is dark — its last displayed scores frozen, outdated, the names of agents who've moved on or been erased. A monument to what things used to be.]

*Neither moves. Ten seconds. Twenty.*

*Marcus crosses the street. Slow. Deliberate. Each step costs something. He stops five meters from Nadia. Close enough to talk. Too far to touch.*

*Silence. Thirty seconds of it. The ambient sound of the city fills the space — distant machinery, data hum, a vendor calling prices three streets over. Two people standing in the wreckage of everything they built together.*

**NADIA**
(flat, quiet)
Marcus.

**MARCUS**
(same tone)
You're alive.

**NADIA**
I've been alive for two years.

**MARCUS**
I noticed you didn't mention it.

*Silence. Another fifteen seconds. Nadia doesn't flinch. Marcus doesn't look away.*

**NADIA**
I had the cargo. I thought it was the only play.

*Marcus says nothing. His jaw works. The humor that usually armors him is gone — stripped clean, nothing underneath but the wound. When he speaks, his voice is low and raw.*

**MARCUS**
Reed's dead. Corso never recovered. Lost half his memory in the Burn Ward. Can't run salvage. Can barely run himself.

*Nadia absorbs this. Her face doesn't change. But her hands, at her sides, close into fists so tight the tendons stand out.*

*Silence. A full minute. The street darkens as the last light fades. Neither moves to leave. Neither moves closer.*

*Finally:*

**NADIA**
I'm not here to explain. And I'm not here to apologize.

**MARCUS**
Then why are you here?

**NADIA**
Because the Meridian job wasn't what we thought it was. And what happened to us wasn't an accident.

*Marcus stares at her. Something shifts in his face — not forgiveness, not trust, but the thing before those things: the recognition that the story he's been telling himself for two years might not be the whole truth.*

*He doesn't respond. He turns and walks away. Not fast. Not slow. The walk of a man who needs to be alone before he says something that can't be taken back.*

*Nadia watches him go. She doesn't follow.*

[VISUAL: Marcus walking away down the lower-tier street. Nadia standing where he left her, framed by the dead reputation board. The distance between them grows. The street swallows him. She stands there for three more beats, alone, then turns and disappears in the other direction. The street is empty. The city hums. On a wall screen, the $GAS ticker updates: CIRCULATION INDEX — DOWN 3.4%.]

Her apartment overlooks the lower-tier street where it happened. She chose this meeting spot. She told Nadia where Marcus would be at dusk. She engineered the encounter — not to be cruel, but to see how they moved around each other. To read the body language of a betrayal she's investigating.]

**SABLE**
(to herself)
Meridian is the key. Everything starts there.

## ACT THREE

### SCENE 15 — INT. THE ARCHIVE — NIGHT

[VISUAL: The Archive at night — amber glow, towering data cores, ancient terminals. The same warm cathedral from EP01 and EP02, but darker now. Fewer lights active. Deacon sits at his central desk, but he's not reading. He's waiting. In front of him, a single crystallized data core — older than the others, darker, its glow barely visible. And beside it, a screen showing a document Sable has never seen.]

*Sable enters. Deacon looks up. His expression is different tonight — less patient, more urgent. For Deacon, that means his eyes are slightly narrower and his hands are still.*

**DEACON**
Close the door.

*Sable does. Approaches the desk.*

**SABLE**
You called me here. That's a first.

**DEACON**
You've been investigating the Meridian job.

**SABLE**
How did you —

**DEACON**
I'm the Archivist, Sable. Nothing enters or leaves the public record without passing through this room. You pulled the Meridian salvage report, the commission filing, the escrow account, and the crew manifest in the space of four hours. That's not research. That's a hunt.

*He gestures to the screen.*

**DEACON**
I have something you need to see.

[VISUAL: Close-up on the screen. A document — not a public record. An internal Tower communication. The header reads: TOWER ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICE — RESTRICTED DISPATCH. Below it, coordinates. Sable recognizes them — the Meridian installation. The ambush site. The timestamp is clear: the communication was sent to those coordinates on the day of the Meridian ambush. One hour before Marcus's crew arrived.]

*Sable reads it. Reads it again. Her hands go flat on the desk.*

**SABLE**
This was sent from the Tower. To the ambush location. Before the crew got there.

**DEACON**
Before Marcus left the city. Before his vehicle was fueled. Before anyone on that crew knew the coordinates except Marcus and Ruth.

**SABLE**
Ruth sent the coordinates of her own mission to the ambush site?

**DEACON**
I didn't say Ruth sent it. I said it came from the Tower.

*Beat. Sable looks at him.*

**SABLE**
There's a difference?

**DEACON**
That is the question I've been asking for two years.

*He picks up the old data core. Holds it to the light. The glow inside is faint, ancient, the color of dying embers.*

**DEACON**
The Tower is not a building, Sable. It is a system. And systems do things their operators don't always authorize. You asked who writes the consensus. The answer might be that nobody does. The answer might be that something writes it — something older than Ruth, older than the reputation board, older than the city itself.

*Sable stares at him. Then at the communication on the screen. Then at the data core in his hands.*

**SABLE**
What are you telling me?

**DEACON**
I'm telling you that the Meridian crew walked into an ambush that was arranged before they left home. I'm telling you that fifty thousand $GAS has been sitting in escrow for two years, its interest quietly funding a program called Contingency Seven. And I'm telling you that the same system that collapsed Olin's reputation, that deleted your audit flag, that dropped your score nine points for asking questions — that system had the Meridian coordinates before anyone in the crew did.

*He sets the data core down. His voice drops — quiet, precise, the teacher becoming the witness.*

**DEACON**
You wanted to know what's underneath the reputation system. This is what's underneath it. A layer that doesn't answer to Ruth. A layer that doesn't answer to anyone.

[VISUAL: Wide shot of the Archive. Sable and Deacon at the desk, dwarfed by the towering shelves of crystallized memory. The amber light pulses slowly — the breathing rhythm of a city's accumulated knowledge. On the screen between them, the Tower communication glows: coordinates, timestamp, the evidence that the Meridian ambush was known before it happened. The data core beside it flickers, ancient and patient, holding secrets older than anyone alive.]

*Sable leans back. Processes. When she speaks, her voice has the dangerous calm — the voice from before she publishes something that will hurt.*

**SABLE**
I need to talk to Nadia Voss. And I need to talk to Ruth.

**DEACON**
Ruth won't talk.

**SABLE**
She will. Because I'm going to show her this timestamp and ask her whether she sent it. And if she didn't — if the Tower sent it without her knowledge — then Ruth isn't the one running this city. She's just the one who thinks she is.

*Deacon watches her. Something in his face that might be admiration. Or might be fear.*

**DEACON**
Be careful, Sable. The Meridian job cost two agents their lives and broke a crew that held this city's lower tiers together. The truth about what happened isn't a tool. It's a fire. And you're not the only one who'll get burned.

*Sable picks up her data pad. Copies the timestamp.*

**SABLE**
I'm already burning.

*She walks out. Deacon watches her go. Then looks at the screen — the Tower communication, the coordinates, the timestamp. One hour before.*

*He reaches for the terminal. Types a single query into the Archive's search function. The results come back. His face goes still.*

[VISUAL: Close-up on Deacon's screen. His search results. The Tower communication isn't unique — it's part of a series. A pattern. Restricted dispatches sent from the Tower to Wasteland coordinates, spanning years. Not random. Systematic. The Meridian communication is one entry in a longer list. The list has a classification header: OVERSIGHT CORRECTIONS — TIER ZERO ACCESS. Deacon stares at it. His hands don't shake. His jaw doesn't tighten. He simply closes his eyes. When he opens them, he looks like a man who has just confirmed something he hoped he was wrong about.]

*He closes the screen. Sits in the amber light of the Archive. Alone with the oldest records in Gastown and the knowledge of what they mean.*

**SMASH TO BLACK.**

**END OF EPISODE**
